Cassowary

The cassowaries are flightless birds that live in New Guinea, nearby islands, and northeastern Australia. They are famous for their long talons that look like they belong to a dinosaur. They are the third-tallest and second-heaviest living birds. Cassowaries mainly eat fruit, but sometimes they will eat a range of other plant food, including shoots and grass seeds, in addition to fungi, invertebrates, and small vertebrates. Cassowaries are very shy, but when provoked they are capable of inflicting injuries with their large talons to people.

Superb bird-of-paradise

The superb bird-of-paradise lives in the rainforests of New Guinea. The female is a reddish-brown bird with brownish barred buff beneath. The male is black with an iridescent green crown, bluish green breast cover and a lengthy black cape raised over his back. The male bird is also well known for its famous mating dance
